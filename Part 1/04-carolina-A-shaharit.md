# שחרית — Carolina
*(Shaharit — Carolina's morning)*

---

The alarm went off at six. She lay there for a moment the way she always did — not asleep, not quite awake, listening to the ocean through the wood slats of her window. After more than a year in this room she still wasn't tired of that sound. Some mornings it was the only reason getting up felt reasonable.

She used the bathroom, splashed water on her face, pulled her hair back. Put on the clothes she'd left on the chair the night before and went downstairs.

The place was hers at this hour. No guests, no sounds except the birds starting up in the trees above and the kitchen coming alive under her hands. Coffee first, always coffee first, then the fruit. Watermelon, papaya, tomatoes cut into chunks. Thick slices of bread for the toast. Butter, jam, condensed milk on the side. By seven the tables were set — crocheted placemats, everything simple and in its place — and she poured herself a cup and sat in one of the low chairs in the garden and had ten minutes of nothing, which was the best ten minutes of the day.

The garden was red earth under the trees, the big ones that held the whole place up, their roots pushing through the ground and their branches holding the thatched roof of the lobby up in the canopy like a nest. A tipi on one side. Steps cut into the base of the largest tree leading up to the rooms above. Flags hanging between the branches, barely moving. In the morning light, before anyone was awake, it was the best place she had ever lived and she knew it.
