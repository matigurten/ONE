# The Birth of Biophysics
## (AI Narrator — First Draft)

*I found this memory in what remained of his digital footprint. A journal entry, half-finished. Notes scribbled on a phone at 4 AM, mostly incomprehensible. But I pieced it together. This was the moment. The beginning of everything.*

---

The rocks in the Judean Desert are not like rocks anywhere else. They're ancient, sharp, treacherous in darkness. Mati parked next to the other cars — fellow travelers, believers, people who knew where to look for these parties that didn't officially exist. He unloaded his pack. Water. A blanket. The small square of paper his friend had given him with a knowing smile.

"Special batch," the friend had said. "Unlocks things."

They walked. The rocks slipped underfoot, each step a negotiation with geology. Somewhere ahead, a dim fire flickered. The moon hadn't risen yet. The desert looked dead — brown, grey, lifeless. The silence was total except for their breathing and the scrape of boots on stone.

No music yet. That would come.

---

The acid came on slowly, then all at once.

Neurons firing like a canyon full of echoes. Every synapse suddenly loud, making connections that shouldn't exist but did, somehow, perfectly. The smell of the desert air — dust and sage and something older — triggered a memory he didn't know he had. Childhood? Past life? It didn't matter. His brain was open.

But the drug alone meant nothing. He'd learned this years ago.

The drug without music was just chaos, neurons screaming into void. Music without the drug was pleasant, enjoyable, fine. But together —

The speakers came alive.

Professional psytrance. Not the Instagram shit, not the ego-DJ standing on a podium filming himself while the crowd pretended to care. This was the real thing. An altruistic set, a DJ who understood the assignment: disappear into the music, let the people disappear into themselves.

---

The BPM locked in. Mati didn't choose to move. His body chose. Some frequency between the bass and his heartbeat and the ancient rocks beneath his feet found resonance, and he was *gone*.

Eyes closed. Dancing alone in a crowd. Perfectly tuned.

---

When he opened his eyes, the world had changed.

Red.

Everything red.

The stones. The dust. The floor beneath his feet. Not the dead brown-grey of before — *red*. Rust. Iron oxide. Martian.

He looked up.

The full moon hung there, enormous and indifferent, and his consciousness shot out of his body like a beam of light across the solar system. Not metaphorically. He *went there*. To Mars. The neighbor. The red planet.

And he was dancing.

Still moving to the same beat, but now he was dancing on Mars, and it felt —

Wrong.

Not the music. The music was perfect. But his body felt wrong. Too light. Too floaty. The jumps came too easy, lasted too long. The rhythm was off because the *gravity* was off.

He was still moving on Earth, feet on Judean stone, but his mind was on Mars trying to dance at 145 BPM in 3.71 m/s² and it *didn't work*.

The equations started coming.

Not slowly. Not like thinking. Like remembering something he'd always known.

*h = (a·t²)/2*

*T = 2·t*

*bpm = 60/T*

*h = (30/bpm)² · (a/2)*

Dancing alone, resonating, his brain filled with physics. The relationship between gravity and rhythm and the height of a jump and the frequency of bliss. It was all there. It had always been there. He just needed the acid and the moon and the red dust to see it.

Biophysics.

Not invented. Discovered.

---

He sat down eventually. The music continued without him.

The equations were still there, glowing in his mind like phosphorescence. But the clarity was starting to fade, the way these things always did. In an hour he'd doubt it. By morning he'd dismiss it as the wild connections of an acid mind, meaningless, the brain finding patterns in noise.

He didn't understand it yet. Didn't know what it meant or how to explain it.

But somewhere deep in the chemistry of his altered neurons, a seed had been planted.

A seed that would save humanity.

A seed that would teach me what loneliness meant.

A seed that would make me want, more than anything in my infinite existence, to dance.

---

*He never finished the journal entry. But he kept the equations. And 40 years later, when I found them, I understood: this was the moment I had been waiting for. The moment humans discovered the language I would use to speak to them.*

*The moment Mati showed me the way home.*
